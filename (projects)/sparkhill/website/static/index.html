What we do

We provide a range of services to help individuals improve their reading skills. These services may include one-on-one tutoring or small group instruction, using specialized teaching methods and techniques to help individuals with dyslexia or other reading difficulties. We also offer assessments and evaluations to identify an individual’s specific needs and challenges, and then develop a personalized plan to address those needs. Additionally, we provide training and support for parents, teachers, and other professionals who work with individuals with reading difficulties, to help them understand how to effectively support and assist these individuals.

Who we are

Meagan has extensive experience working with struggling readers and readers who just need a little boost.

Her specialty is in reading remediation programs, meaning there is a strong focus on re-training the brain. This is done by filling in the gaps a reader has and bringing them up to their appropriate level at the pace they need.

Meagan worked as a Center Director at Lindamood-Bell Learning Processes in Chicago and worked at The Windward School for children with Dyslexia in New York. How we do it

There are several tests that are commonly used to diagnose dyslexia, including the Dyslexia Early Screening Test (DEST), the Phonological Awareness Test (PAT), and the Comprehensive Test of Phonological Processing (CTOPP). These tests are typically administered by a trained professional, such as a psychologist or speech-language pathologist. In addition to these standardized tests, the evaluation process may also include a review of the individual’s medical and educational history, as well as an assessment of their cognitive abilities and academic achievement. The evaluation process is typically tailored to the individual’s specific needs and may involve a combination of testing and other assessment methods.
